Safety Concepts

know these at multiple articulation levels

the 4 articulation levels
  1. 5-year-old: Metaphor anyone can understand
  2. Colleague: Clear explanation for a smart non-expert
  3. Technical: Precise terminology, can discuss tradeoffs
  4. Expert: Open problems, current research, your views

tier 1: must know cold

Transformers

5-year-old: "It's like a detective that looks at all the words at once to figure out which ones are most important."

Technical: "Self-attention computes weighted combinations of value vectors, where weights come from query-key dot products."

RLHF

5-year-old: "We train the AI to do things humans think are good, like training a dog with treats."

Technical: "SFT → Reward Model → PPO. The reward model learns preference scores from human feedback."

Limitations: Sycophancy, reward hacking, Goodhart's Law.

Mechanistic Interpretability

5-year-old: "Opening up the AI's brain to see which parts light up when it thinks."

Challenges: Polysemanticity, superposition, scaling to frontier models.

Scalable Oversight

5-year-old: "How do you check if a very smart helper did good work on homework you don't understand?"

Approaches: Debate, recursive reward modeling, weak-to-strong generalization.

tier 2: should know well

Constitutional AI

Self-improvement through critique based on principles.

Induction Heads

Attention heads that implement in-context learning.

Superposition

Models encode more features than dimensions.

SAEs

Sparse Autoencoders for disentangling features.
