Daily Rhythm

a sustainable structure for learning while working full-time

This schedule runs on consistency over intensity. Following a predictable rhythm builds habits and ensures steady progress without burnout. The structure assumes you're working full-time and have a life outside of study.

the principle

Plan for the lower end (~14 hours/week) and treat extra time as a bonus. Some weeks you'll hit 20 hours; some weeks life happens and you get 12. Both are fine. The timeline has buffer built in.

weekday mornings

30 min anki + outreach

morning block

First 15 min    Anki review (all due cards)
Last 15 min     LinkedIn content OR networking outreach

Mon/Wed/Fri     Write or polish content
Tue/Thu         Send 1-2 networking messages

weekday evenings

1-2 hours technical learning (flexible)

real life first

Your study block starts after you've handled gym, dinner, and life. Typically 7:30-8pm onwards. Some nights you'll have 2 hours, some nights just 1. That's fine.

evening block

Full evening (2 hrs):
  First 5 min      Review yesterday's notes
  Next 80-90 min   Active learning — videos/coding/reading
  Next 15-20 min   Process: Analog → Digital notes + Anki
  Last 5 min       Preview tomorrow's content

Short evening (1 hr):
  First 5 min      Review yesterday's notes
  Next 45-50 min   Active learning (focused single topic)
  Last 5-10 min    Quick Anki card creation (2-3 cards)

weekend deep work

6-8 hours total across two days

saturday

4-6 hrs
  • 2-3 hour deep work blocks with breaks
  • Work on weekly project/implementation
  • Process accumulated notes
  • Catch up on missed content

sunday

2-4 hrs
  • Full Anki review (all cards)
  • Interview prep ritual (60 min)
  • Content creation (blog post draft)
  • Weekly review + plan next week

weekly review template

Every Sunday, answer these questions:

  1. What can I now explain that I couldn't last week?
  2. What's still fuzzy?
  3. What debugging stories do I have?
  4. Am I on track?
  5. Energy check: Sustainable or burning out?
